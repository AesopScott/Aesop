How AI Learns — Training, Data & Models

Explore how machine learning works and why data is everything.

◆ Module 2 of 10

◆ Section 1

The Training Process

Machine learning models learn by being shown enormous amounts of examples and adjusting their internal parameters to get better at predicting outcomes. This process — called training — can take weeks and consume millions of dollars of computing power for the largest models.

◆ Section 2

What Is a Neural Network

Modern AI is built on neural networks — systems loosely inspired by the brain, consisting of layers of mathematical operations that transform inputs into outputs. The 'deep' in deep learning refers to the many layers stacked together.

◆ Section 3

Why Data Quality Matters

A model is only as good as its training data. Biased data produces biased models. Incomplete data produces models with blind spots. The curation and quality of training data is one of the most important and least discussed aspects of AI development.

◆ Section 4

Supervised vs Unsupervised Learning

In supervised learning, models are trained on labeled examples. In unsupervised learning, models find patterns without labels. Most modern AI uses a combination — including reinforcement learning from human feedback, which is how language models are fine-tuned to be helpful.

◆ Section 5

Model Size and Capability

Larger models with more parameters generally perform better — but not always. The relationship between scale and capability is complex. Smaller, well-trained models often outperform larger, poorly-trained ones on specific tasks.
