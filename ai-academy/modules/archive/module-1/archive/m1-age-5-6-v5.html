What Is AI?

Mia finds a helper hiding in her mom's phone — and meets a little robot who has never, ever tasted a cookie.

Page 1 of 1 — What Is AI?

Mia was getting ready for school when her mom's phone made a noise.

"Mom! What was that?"

"That's the weather helper," said Mom. "It says bring your umbrella today — rain is coming!"

Mia stared at the phone. "But how does the phone know about rain?"

Mom smiled. "That's called AI. Want to find out what it is?"

"Yes!" said Mia. "Is it magic?"

"No," said Mom. "It's actually much cooler than magic."

At school, Mia met a little screen on wheels named ZIPP.

"Hi Mia! I am an AI!" said ZIPP.

"What can you do?" Mia asked.

"I can answer questions! I can help you find things! I can tell you the weather!" said ZIPP.

Mia thought hard. "Can you feel happy?"

ZIPP paused. "No. I do not feel things."

"Can you taste food?"

"No," said ZIPP.

"Have you ever tasted a cookie?"

"Never," said ZIPP.

Mia's eyes went wide. "That is the saddest thing I have ever heard."

"ZIPP," said Mia, "how did you learn to answer things?"

"People taught me," said ZIPP. "They showed me lots and lots of words. And pictures. And sounds. Then I learned what things are called and how things work."

"Like going to school?" said Mia.

"Yes! Exactly like that. Except," said ZIPP, "I learned MUCH faster. And I had to be taught everything. I did not know anything at all when I was first turned on."

"Not even your own name?"

"Not even my own name," said ZIPP.

Mia nodded slowly. "So the people who taught you — they're kind of like your parents?"

ZIPP blinked three times, which was ZIPP's way of smiling. "I never thought of it that way. But yes. A little bit."

"Can AI be in other things?" asked Mia. "Not just robots?"

"Oh yes," said ZIPP. "AI is hiding in lots of places you would not expect."

"Like where?"

"When you talk to a voice helper and it answers you — that is AI."

"Like when I asked it what a penguin sounds like?"

"Yes! And when a music app plays a song you love that it picked just for you — that is AI too. It learned what kinds of songs you like."

Mia's mouth fell open. "The phone has been paying attention to me?"

"It was learning," said ZIPP. "So it could help you better."

Mia looked at her mom's phone differently now.

On the way home, Mia was quiet for a long time.

"Mom," she said. "ZIPP said AI learns from people. So if people teach AI kind things — it learns to be kind?"

"Yes," said Mom.

"And if people teach it mean things — it learns mean things?"

Mom stopped walking. She knelt down. "Mia. That is one of the most important questions anyone can ask about AI."

"So it matters what we put in?" said Mia.

"It matters a lot," said Mom. "The people who make AI have to be very careful. And the people who use AI — they have to be careful too. Maybe even you, someday."

Mia thought about that the whole rest of the way home.

That night she drew a picture of ZIPP — and wrote underneath: Made by people. Taught by people. A little bit like a friend.

✨ Story Lab — Meet a Real AI!

Ask a grown-up to open a voice helper (Siri, Alexa, or Google). Try these together:

  1. Ask: "What sound does a penguin make?" Did it know?
  2. Ask: "Tell me a short story about a penguin." What happened?
  3. Ask: "Do you have feelings?" What does it say?
  4. Talk about it: Was AI helpful? Did anything surprise you? What's one thing AI can do that's pretty amazing? What's one thing YOU can do that AI cannot?
You just did what scientists do — you tried something and paid attention to what happened. That's called an experiment. You're already thinking like a scientist. 🔬

🧪 Where does AI learn everything it knows?

✅ Yes! People teach AI by showing it millions and millions of examples. It cannot learn on its own.
AI learns from people! They show it examples — pictures, words, sounds — over and over until it gets good. It cannot learn anything without human teachers.

🧪 Can AI feel happy or sad?

✅ Right! AI can answer questions and help with things, but it does not feel. No happy, no sad, no excited — and no cookies.
AI does not have feelings. It can say the word "happy" — but it does not actually feel happy. Only living things feel things.
