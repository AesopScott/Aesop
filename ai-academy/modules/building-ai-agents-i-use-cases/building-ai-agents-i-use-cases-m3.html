Unlike a simple search engine that returns links, a research agent formulates sub-queries, evaluates source quality, extracts structured information, resolves conflicts between sources, and produces a coherent output — all without waiting for a human to approve each step.</p> <p>The architecture typically involves a planning layer that breaks a research goal into sub-tasks, a retrieval layer that queries databases, APIs, or the open web, and a synthesis layer that integrates findings. The system issued hundreds of sub-queries per session, synthesized results across dozens of sources, and produced structured reports with inline citations. Internal Microsoft data indicated a 4–6x reduction in time-to-first-draft for complex research tasks compared to unaided analysts.</p> </div> </div> <div class="section-divider"></div> <div class="lesson-section"> <h2 class="section-heading">High-Stakes Domains: Drug Discovery and Finance</h2> <div class="section-body"> <p>The pharmaceutical industry has been an early and aggressive adopter.
