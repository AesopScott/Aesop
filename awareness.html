The Situation

AI isn't coming. It's already here — and it's already sorting.

Every week, tools built on artificial intelligence are being woven into hiring processes, healthcare systems, classrooms, financial decisions, and creative work. Most of the people affected by these tools have never been given a basic explanation of how they work, what they can't do, or who to hold accountable when they go wrong.

"The people who understand AI are mostly the people who built it. Everyone else is navigating it blind."

Your job is changing, whether you know it or not

AI tools are being adopted by employers faster than workers are being trained to use them. Understanding what AI does well — and badly — is becoming a basic professional skill, the way spreadsheets once were.

Your kids are learning with AI tools no one has vetted

Children are already interacting with AI tutors, writing assistants, and content recommendation systems. AI literacy is becoming as essential as reading literacy — and most school curricula haven't caught up.

The hype is obscuring real risks

Every week brings a headline either promising AI will solve everything or warning it will end civilization. Neither is true. The actual picture — algorithmic bias, economic displacement, misinformation at scale — is more important and less dramatic than either extreme.

You don't need to be technical to understand this

You don't need to write code. You need to understand enough to ask the right questions, spot the red flags, and know when to trust an AI-generated output and when to push back. That's what AESOP teaches.
