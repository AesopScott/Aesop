Move to Grow — Exercise for Under 18

Building strength, coordination, and lifelong movement habits for young people.

Module 2 of 10 Under 18 Exercise

◆ Section 1

Why Movement Is Non-Negotiable for Youth

Physical activity in youth builds cardiovascular fitness, bone density, muscular strength, and mental health simultaneously. Children who are physically active have better academic performance, better sleep, better mood regulation, and lower rates of anxiety and depression. The case for youth exercise is overwhelming.

◆ Section 2

The Importance of Unstructured Play

Before age 12, unstructured play — running, climbing, throwing, jumping — is as valuable as organized sport. It builds proprioception, coordination, creativity, and social skills. The over-scheduling of children's time has reduced this critical developmental activity.

◆ Section 3

Sport and Team Activity

Participation in organized sport teaches competition, resilience, teamwork, and discipline alongside physical fitness. The sport itself matters less than consistent participation. Any sport engaged with regularly and joyfully is a good sport.

◆ Section 4

Strength Training for Adolescents

Properly supervised resistance training is safe and beneficial for adolescents. It builds muscle, improves bone density, reduces injury risk in sports, and establishes the habit of strength training before adult life makes it harder to start. The key word is supervised — proper form matters enormously.

◆ Section 5

Screen Time and Movement

The single biggest threat to youth physical activity is sedentary screen time. Every hour of passive screen time is an hour not spent moving. The goal is not to eliminate screens but to protect movement time — ideally at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity every day.
