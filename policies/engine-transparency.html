What the AESOP Engine Is

⚙️ Patent Pending — U.S. Patent and Trademark Office Application #64/018,565

The AESOP Engine is a proprietary, patent-pending curriculum generation system designed to produce story-driven AI literacy course content. It is the technical foundation of the AESOP AI Academy platform, enabling our team to author and update course materials at scale while maintaining the narrative, age-appropriate framing that makes our content distinctive.

The Engine is a curriculum authoring tool — it assists human educators in creating content. It is not an autonomous instructional AI, not a tutoring chatbot, and not a system that interacts directly with learners in real time.

How It Works: Inputs

The Engine takes the following as structured inputs from the curriculum team:

  • Learning objectives — what a learner should be able to do after completing the module
  • Target audience — age group, prior knowledge level, professional context
  • Subject matter domain — AI literacy, AI ethics, cybersecurity, AI governance, etc.
  • Narrative parameters — story characters, setting, tone, and scenario constraints
  • Standards alignment targets — which framework(s) the module should satisfy (AI4K12, NIST, etc.)
No Student Data Used as Input The AESOP Engine does not receive, process, or use any student personal data, behavioral data, or education records as inputs at any stage. Inputs are curriculum design parameters only.

How It Works: Outputs

The Engine produces structured draft content for human review, including:

  • Lesson narrative frameworks and story scripts
  • Module content outlines and learning sequences
  • Assessment question drafts (multiple choice, reflection prompts, scenario exercises)
  • Vocabulary and concept definitions at target reading level

All outputs are in structured text format. They are delivered to the curriculum team for review — they are never automatically published to learners.

No Autonomous Publishing No AI-generated content is published to learners without at least one mandatory stage of human review. This is not a policy goal — it is a technical constraint enforced in our publishing workflow.

Third-Party AI Components

The AESOP Engine utilizes large language model (LLM) APIs as part of its text generation capability. These components are:

  • Accessed under enterprise data agreements that explicitly prohibit training on customer or user data
  • Never given access to student PII — inputs are curriculum design parameters only
  • Used exclusively for curriculum authoring, not for real-time learner interactions
  • Subject to the same human review pipeline as all other content outputs
For Procurement Staff The use of third-party LLM APIs in curriculum authoring is analogous to a teacher using a word processor with AI suggestions — the teacher (and our curriculum team) remains the author and decision-maker. The tool does not independently instruct students.
